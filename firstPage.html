Cognitive Distortions

Have you ever convinced yourself of something that isn’t true? Ever made excuses about yourself that stopped your growth and development in life?

If so, it is important to understand cognitive distortion and what category you fall under.

Cognitive distortions are inaccurate thoughts which are used to strengthen negative thinking.

Let Thought Catcher help you.

There are ten cognitive distortions:

1. 'All or Nothing Mindset

2. Jumping to Conclusions

3. Overgeneralisation

You come to a general conclusion based on a single incident.

Example: you fail an exam, conclude you’re a failure and decide if you should drop out.

4. Catastrophisation

5. Mental Filter

You take the negative details and magnify them while also filtering out all positives of a situation.

Example: you’re a Youtuber and one of your videos attract loads of positive comments from your fans, however, you notice a bad comment and you only focus on that.

6. Disqualifying the Positive

7. Personalisation

8. Shoulds and Oughts

9. Emotional Reasoning

10. Labelling
